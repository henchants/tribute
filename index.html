Hideaki Anno

Hideaki Anno, the creator of Neon Genesis Evangelion

Hideaki Anno is a renowned Japanese animator, director, and screenwriter. He is best known for his work on the iconic anime series "Neon Genesis Evangelion." He was born on May 22, 1960, in Ube, Japan.

Timeline of Anno's Works:

  • 1984: "Nadia: The Secret of Blue Water" (Debut work) - Anno's first major work, "Nadia," was a significant milestone in his career as an animator and director.
  • 1995: "Neon Genesis Evangelion" (TV Series) - "Neon Genesis Evangelion" is one of Anno's most iconic and influential works, setting new standards for the mecha genre.
  • 1997: "The End of Evangelion" (Film) - This film served as a conclusion to the "Neon Genesis Evangelion" series and is highly regarded in the anime community.
  • 2007: "Rebuild of Evangelion" (Film Series) - Anno revisited the "Evangelion" series with a new film series that gained widespread attention.
  • 2016: "Shin Godzilla" (Film) - Anno directed "Shin Godzilla," a reimagining of the classic Japanese kaiju, which was critically acclaimed.
